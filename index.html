About This Project

About This Project

The South African Grid Flexibility Explorer is a systems-mapping tool designed to support understanding of South Africa's electricity network as an interconnected whole.

It was developed as a companion exercise to the research paper Reimagining Grid Flexibility in a Constrained Power System. During development, it became clear that visualizing individual problems in isolation did not adequately explain the underlying dynamics of the electricity system.

The project therefore evolved from a problem visualization exercise into a systems-mapping exercise. Rather than asking how individual problems could be solved, the visualization seeks to understand how generation, transmission, storage, and demand interact to produce both constraints and opportunities.

The dashboard should be viewed as a systems exploration tool rather than an operational model. Its purpose is to support understanding, stimulate discussion, challenge assumptions, and generate new questions for future research.

Methodology

The development process followed seven broad stages:

  1. Read and interpret the article to identify key themes
  2. Identify key challenges relating to grid flexibility
  3. Generate preliminary solution concepts
  4. Recognize gaps in system understanding
  5. Build a system map of existing infrastructure
  6. Explore relationships through visualization
  7. Separate mapping from solution development

To avoid premature conclusions, solution discussions were separated from the visualization process. Version 1 therefore focuses exclusively on understanding the current system structure.

Key Observations

Several observations emerged during the mapping process:

  • The system appears more connected than expected. Decisions in one component influence outcomes elsewhere.
  • Flexibility is a system property, not a single technology. It emerges from interactions between multiple components.
  • Geography matters. The spatial distribution of assets introduces constraints and opportunities not immediately visible in traditional reports.
  • Energy flow may be as important as energy production. A central question became: "How effectively can energy move through the system when and where it is needed?"

Limitations

This visualization has several important limitations:

  • Not an operational model. The dashboard is not intended to simulate actual grid operations.
  • Simplified relationships. Many real-world variables have been omitted to maintain clarity.
  • Data constraints. Asset locations and relationships are represented at a high level. Some BESS locations are approximate.
  • Exploratory purpose. The objective is understanding rather than prediction. The visualization should be interpreted as a conceptual systems exploration.

Future Development

Future versions may include additional layers and analytical capabilities:

  • Capacity and utilization rate overlays
  • Congestion indicators and transmission loading
  • Temporal analysis: daily patterns, seasonal variation, peak vs off-peak
  • Scenario testing: renewable expansion, storage deployment, demand response
  • Policy analysis: infrastructure investment priorities, flexibility market design

Data Sources

Infrastructure coordinates are sourced from:

  • Global Energy Monitor (GEM) — exact WGS 84 coordinates for major power stations
  • Eskom public records — BESS Phase 1 locations
  • REIPPPP records — renewable energy project data
  • Public geographic data — city centres for demand nodes
